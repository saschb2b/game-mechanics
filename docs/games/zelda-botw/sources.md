# Sources

## Dev interviews & talks

- **GDC 2017 panel** — *Change and Constant: Breaking Conventions with The Legend of Zelda: Breath of the Wild*. Hidemaro Fujibayashi (director), Satoru Takizawa (art director), Takuhiro Dohta (technical director). The canonical articulation of "additive vs multiplicative" design and the chemistry engine.
  [GDC Vault talk on YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=QyMsF31NdNc) ·
  [Game Developer write-up](https://www.gamedeveloper.com/design/video-designing-i-zelda-breath-of-the-wild-i-s-unconventional-mechanics) ·
  [Thumbsticks summary](https://www.thumbsticks.com/gdc-17-breath-of-the-wild-science-lies/)
- [**Niche Gamer — Fujibayashi explains lack of traditional dungeons**](https://nichegamer.com/zelda-breath-of-the-wild-director-explains-the-lack-of-traditional-dungeons-why-the-shrines-are-short/) — direct quote on the 60 km² ÷ shrine-density math.
- [**NintendoSoup — Fujibayashi explains why dungeons gave way to shrines**](https://nintendosoup.com/breath-wild-director-explains-dungeons-gave-way-shrines/) — companion piece, with the Divine Beasts compromise framing.
- [**Nintendo Everything — Devs on Shrines and Divine Beasts, lack of long dungeons**](https://nintendoeverything.com/zelda-breath-of-the-wild-devs-on-the-thinking-behind-shrines-and-divine-beasts-lack-of-long-dungeons/) — additional context on the team's reasoning.
- [**Inverse — Aonuma on weapon durability for BotW2**](https://www.inverse.com/gaming/breath-of-the-wild-2-weapon-durability) — Aonuma's defense of breakable weapons + the strategic-use framing.
- [**Inverse — Tears of the Kingdom aims to fix BotW's most controversial system**](https://www.inverse.com/gaming/zelda-tears-kingdom-weapon-durability) — coverage of the Fuse mechanic as the team's correction.
- [**ScreenRant — Why You Can't Repair Weapons in BotW**](https://screenrant.com/breath-wild-repair-weapon-durability/) — concise summary of Aonuma's stated rationale.
- [**Zelda Dungeon — Inspiration Behind the Divine Beasts**](https://www.zeldadungeon.net/breath-of-the-wilds-developers-reveal-the-inspiration-behind-the-divine-beasts/) — devs on the moving-dungeon / gravity-dungeon design.
