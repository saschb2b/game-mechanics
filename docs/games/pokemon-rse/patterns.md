# Patterns Pokémon Ruby/Sapphire/Emerald exemplifies

| Pattern | How RSE uses it |
|---|---|
| [`loadout-as-budget`](/concepts/loadout-as-budget) | At two grains: (a) the **4-move moveset** per Pokémon — 4 slots, hundreds of options, type-tagged — the smallest-grain canonical budget in this knowledge base; (b) the **510 EVs across 6 stats** per Pokémon — 252 cap per stat forces ~2-stat focus. |
| [`bonus-with-drawback`](/concepts/bonus-with-drawback) | **Natures** (+10% one stat / −10% another, 25 fixed grid). **Choice items** (+50% Attack but locked to one move). **Macho Brace** (2× EV gain, halved Speed). **Toxic Orb / Flame Orb** (badly poisons / burns the holder, but enables Guts / Quick Feet / Poison Heal abilities). The pattern is most-built-out here of any game in the knowledge base. |
| [`late-introduced-mechanics`](/concepts/late-introduced-mechanics) | **Battle Frontier** opens post-credits (~25h in). **Contests** require travelling to Verdanturf or Slateport. **National Dex** unlocks only after beating the game + completing Hoenn Dex + talking to Birch. **Trade-only evolutions** are gated by social interaction. |
| `type-rps-matrix` | The 17-type chart with dual-type multiplication. The canonical instance of asymmetric N-way RPS in commercial games — referenced and rebuilt in countless monster-collectors since. |
| `permanent-stat-profile` | Each Pokémon has a fixed Nature + Ability + IVs (0–31 per stat) set at capture. Profile is largely immutable; the *Pokémon you have is the Pokémon you have.* This drives the breeding / re-rolling competitive subloop. |
| `version-exclusive-trade-gate` | Ruby and Sapphire have asymmetric encounter pools, antagonist-team prominence, and mascot legendary. Trade is required to complete the Pokédex. The trade requirement is the social loop. |
| `third-version-canonical-merge` | Emerald is the canonical edition of Hoenn — merges the two antagonist teams' arcs, makes Rayquaza the mascot, replaces Battle Tower with Battle Frontier, animates sprites. Sold as a separate $30 cartridge ~22 months after R/S. |
| `post-game-challenge-tower` | **Battle Frontier** — 7 facilities, each with its own ruleset (Tournament, Rentals, Auto-Pilot, Score-Judged, Roguelike Pyramid, Random-Door Pike, Standard Tower). 14 Symbols total. The blueprint for every Pokémon endgame since. |
| `weather-as-global-modifier` | Drought / Drizzle / Sand Stream as *abilities* that summon permanent weather. Weather boosts and weakens specific types and triggers ability interactions (Chlorophyll +Speed in sun, Swift Swim +Speed in rain). The basis of three full generations of "Weather Wars" competitive play. |
| `parallel-meta-system` | **Contests, Secret Bases, Berry growing** — three independent progression systems that don't feed the badge / level main loop. Each has its own currency, judges, and completion arc. The franchise's first attempt at "more verbs alongside the main loop." |
| `flavor-as-meta-narrative` | Every move has a battle role AND a contest category — the same 4-slot moveset is read two ways (combat-effective vs presentation). Soft thematic richness for free. |
