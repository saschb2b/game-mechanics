## Where this game sits in the bigger pattern graph

Mina is most strongly linked, via shared patterns, to **[Hollow Knight](/games/hollow-knight/)** (the `opportunity-cost-loadout` charm-budget lineage, plus the fragile-protagonist deliberate combat), to **[Slay the Spire](/games/slay-the-spire/relics)** and **[Hades](/games/hades/boons-and-gods)** (the `bonus-with-drawback` commit-or-collect axis), and to **[Nier: Automata](/games/nier-automata/chip-system)** (the death-drop / retrieval loop, which Mina makes *graduated*).

The unique angles Mina adds to the graph:

- **The plane-shifting dodge** (`burrow-dodge`) — a single verb that is dodge + traversal + opener because it moves *under* the threat layer, not across it. No other entry on the site does this.
- **The buffered death stake** (`graduated-death-penalty`) — Sparks make the first mistakes cheap and only sustained failure expensive, with a level-up mercy rule. A softer, more legible bloodstain than the Souls/Nier originals.
- **The diegetic stat screen** (`diegetic-stats-newspaper`) — the most thoroughly *in-world* progress UI on the site; the closest cousin is Nier's [HUD-as-software](/games/nier-automata/chip-system) but Mina's is print-fiction rather than system-fiction.

Four patterns above (`burrow-dodge`, `graduated-death-penalty`, `diegetic-stats-newspaper`, `retro-constraint-aesthetic`) are candidates for promotion to curated concept pages once a second game in the repo exemplifies them clearly. `currency-as-progression` overlaps enough with the existing [`currency-as-crafting`](/concepts/currency-as-crafting) that it may fold into a broader "currency design" page rather than earn its own.
